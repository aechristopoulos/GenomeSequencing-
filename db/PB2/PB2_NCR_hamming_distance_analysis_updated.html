PB2 H1N1 Forward 1.2 primer NCR hamming distances

PB2_H1_F1 <- read.csv("hamming_distance_results/PB2_H1_PB2_Forward_1.2_results_NCR.csv")

PB2_H1_1 <- PB2_H1_F1[-c(2418, 2419, 2420),]

barplot(table(PB2_H1_1$hamming_distance), main = "PB2 H1N1 hamming distances, forward 1.2 primer", xlab = "Hamming Distance", ylab = "Number of Sequences", ylim = c(0,2500))

PB2 Forward 1.2 primer: CGA CTA GCA AAA GCA GGT CAA ATA TAT TCA

Melting temperature: 57.2ºC

Hairpins (ΔG < -1): 0

Self-dimers (ΔG < -6): 1

Heterodimers (ΔG < -6): 1

This primer is specific for the PB2 segment of H1N1, H5Nx H7N9, and H9N2 strains of Influenza A. The nucleotide differences from the H3Nx strains is noted in italics. The primer includes a 5 nucleotide long 5’ tail (italized) that is not based on the sequences in order to increase the melting temperature and improve other factors of the primer; this tail was not included in the hamming distance calculations. The rest of the primer consists of the universal non-coding region (AGC AAA AGC AGG, bolded), and part of the non-coding region that is unique to the PB2 segments. Based on the alignment shade threshold and hamming distance calculations, 83% of PB2 H1N1 sequences will have a hamming distance of 0 with this primer. 2,417 H1N1 sequences were used for this alignment and calculations.
