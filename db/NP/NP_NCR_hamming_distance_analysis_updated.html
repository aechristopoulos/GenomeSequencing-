NP H1N1 Forward 1.6 primer NCR hamming distances

NP_H1_F1 <- read.csv("hamming_distance_results/NP_H1_NP_Forward_1.6_results_NCR.csv")

NP_H1_1 <- NP_H1_F1[-c(2657, 2658, 2659),]

barplot(table(NP_H1_1$hamming_distance), main = "NP H1N1 hamming distances, forward 1.6 primer", xlab = "Hamming Distance", ylab = "Number of Sequences", ylim = c(0,2500))

NP Forward 1.6 primer: AGC AAA AGC AGG GTA GAT AAT CAC TCA

Melting temperature: 58C

Hairpins (G < -1): 0

Self-dimers (G < -6): 0

Heterodimers (G < -6): 0

This primer is specific for the NP segment of H1N1, H5Nx, H7N9, and H9N2 strains of Influenza A. The nucleotide differences from H3Nx strains are noted in italics. The primer consists of the universal non-coding region (AGC AAA AGC AGG, bolded), and the rest of the non-coding region that is unique to the NP segments. Based on the alignment shade threshold and hamming distance calculations, 88% of NP H1N1 sequences will have a hamming distance of 0 with this primer. 2,656 H1N1 sequences were used for this alignment and calculations.

NP Reverse 2.5 primer NCR hamming distances

NP_H1_R <- read.csv("hamming_distance_results/NP_H1_NP_Reverse_2.5_results_NCR.csv")

NP_H1_3 <- NP_H1_R[-c(2567),]

NP Reverse 2.5 primer: AGG CGA CTA GTA GAA ACA AGG GTA TTT TT

Reverse compliment (without 5 tail): AA AAA TAC CCT TGT TTC TAC T

Melting temperature: 57.9C

Hairpins (G < -1): 0

Self-dimers (G < -6): 1

Heterodimers (G < -6): 0 (with all forward primers)

This primer is universal for all MP segments of all strains of Influenza A. The reverse compliment of this primer was used for the alignments and hamming distance calculations. The primer includes a 8 nucleotide long 5 tail (italized) that is not based on the sequences in order to increase the melting temperature and improve other factors of the primer; this tail was not included in the hamming distance calculations. The rest of the primer consists of the universal non-coding region (AGT AGA AAC AAG G, bolded) and part of the non-coding region that is unique to the NP segments. Based on the alignment shade threshold and hamming distance calculations, 99% of NP H1N1 sequences will have a hamming distance of 0 with this primer. 2,656 H1N1 sequences were used for this alignment and calculations.

In total, 7,336 sequences were used to design the MP primers. These sequences were downloaded from the NCBI Influenza database and the GSAID Influenza database. They were chosen based on length (had to include the non-coding regions) and quality of the sequence. I believe that using the Forward 1.7 primer (mixed-base) and Reverse 2.5 primer pair would be best. However, we could also do a three primer pool with an equal concentration of Forward 1.6 and Forward 1.8, and Reverse 2.5.
